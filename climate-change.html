🌍 Climate Change

Understanding one of the greatest global challenges and how the GCC is building a more resilient future.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term changes in Earth's temperatures and weather patterns. While natural factors influence climate, human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial emissions have significantly accelerated global warming.

Major Causes

• Greenhouse gas emissions
• Burning coal, oil and natural gas
• Deforestation
• Industrial pollution
• Unsustainable consumption

Effects on the GCC

GCC countries face rising temperatures, water scarcity, desertification, sea-level rise, and increasing demand for sustainable infrastructure. These challenges are driving investments in renewable energy and climate adaptation.

How the GCC is Responding

Countries across the Gulf are investing in solar power, green hydrogen, sustainable cities, carbon reduction, and environmental conservation through long-term national visions.

Did You Know?

🌱 Saudi Arabia and the UAE are among the world's fastest-growing investors in renewable energy and green technologies, while Oman is expanding its green hydrogen sector.
